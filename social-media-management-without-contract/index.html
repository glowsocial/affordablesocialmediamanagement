Why Agencies Need Contracts (And AI Services Don't)

Understanding why contracts exist helps you spot the services that genuinely don't need them.

Traditional agencies require long contracts because their onboarding is expensive. Hiring a copywriter, briefing a designer, setting up tracking, and learning your brand voice takes 4–8 weeks of paid staff time. If you cancel after one month, they've lost money. The contract protects their investment.

AI-powered services don't have this problem. Onboarding is automated — you share your website, answer a few questions about your brand, and the AI learns your voice in hours. There's no staff time to recoup. The result: month-to-month work, because the service quality has to earn your renewal every single month.

If a service requires a long contract, ask yourself: what are they afraid will happen if they let me cancel easily?

What to Ask Before Signing Anything

What is the minimum commitment period?

The answer should be "one month" or "none." If they say anything longer, ask why — and whether there's a shorter trial option.

Is there a cancellation fee or early termination penalty?

Some agencies charge 50–100% of the remaining contract balance if you cancel early. This can mean thousands of dollars to exit a bad relationship. Always get the cancellation terms in writing before signing.

Who owns the content created during the engagement?

You should own all content created for your business. Some agencies retain rights to graphics or copy they create, which means you can't use those assets after you cancel. Get IP ownership confirmed before signing.

What specifically happens to my social accounts if I cancel?

Make sure they disconnect cleanly without taking content with them, that your passwords remain yours, and that they don't retain admin access to your accounts. Reputable services handle this gracefully — ask anyway.
