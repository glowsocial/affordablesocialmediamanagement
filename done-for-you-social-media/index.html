Done-For-You Social Media: What It Means and What It Costs

"Done for you" is thrown around loosely in marketing. Here's what it should actually mean — and how to tell if a service is really handling it, or just moving the work around.

Definition

Done-for-you social media means the service handles content creation (caption writing + image creation), platform formatting, scheduling, and publishing — and your only job is to review and approve a batch of posts once a month. If you're still writing the captions, it's not truly done for you.

What "Done For You" Actually Requires

The term is overused. Here's a quick test: if a service requires any of the following from you on a recurring basis, it is not done for you:

❌ Not Done-For-You

  • You write the captions, they schedule
  • You provide the photos, they post
  • You approve a strategy, then do the execution
  • Monthly "content calls" where you brainstorm
  • You handle image sizing for each platform

✅ Actually Done-For-You

  • AI or team writes captions in your voice
  • Images created or sourced automatically
  • Platform formatting handled for you
  • Posts scheduled and published automatically
  • You only review — never create

How AI Made Done-For-You Affordable

Before 2024, true done-for-you social media required a human team: a copywriter, a graphic designer, a social media manager, and a scheduler. That's why agencies charged $2,000–$5,000/month.

AI automation changed the economics completely:

  • Caption writing: AI learns your voice from your website and generates on-brand captions at scale
  • Image creation: AI generates custom images matched to each post's content
  • Platform formatting: Automatic resizing and formatting for each network
  • Scheduling: Posts go out at optimal times without any manual input
